On my way home, I noticed two people communicating through sign language. They were excitedly pointing out a small mouse perched on the shoulder of a man riding a motorbike. It was a beautiful, surreal moment that felt completely detached from the ordinary rush of the city.

Watching them, I wondered how they express intense emotions—how would they argue or debate using only their hands But then I realized perhaps they understand each other better than the rest of us. While spoken words are often loaded with hidden meanings and ambiguity, sign language is direct and clearly defined. The sheer effort they put into each gesture carries a level of sincerity that leaves little room for misunderstanding. In their silence, there is a transparency that words often fail to capture.
